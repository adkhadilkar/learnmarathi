## Story 1: ससा आणि कासव (The Hare and the Tortoise)

**Page 1:** A fast, confident rabbit with long ears running through a lush green forest. Trees and grass around him. Bright, cheerful style.

**Page 2:** Close-up of the rabbit's face showing speed and pride. Wind lines suggest fast movement. Very energetic.

**Page 3:** A slow, patient tortoise with a shell, walking methodically on the same forest path. Very determined expression.

**Page 4:** The rabbit standing confidently at a starting line, challenging the slow tortoise. Both animals look at each other, the rabbit seems cocky.

**Page 5:** A race flag drops or a leaf falls - the race is starting! Both animals begin moving.

**Page 6:** The rabbit is far ahead, running proudly. Ahead is a large shady tree with wide branches.

**Page 7:** The rabbit sleeps peacefully under the tree, curled up, while the tortoise plods along in the background, getting closer.

**Page 8:** The tortoise crosses the finish line with determination, while the rabbit is still sleeping.

**Page 9:** The rabbit wakes up, shocked! The tortoise is ahead. The rabbit realizes slow and steady wins. Both animals look satisfied.

## Story 2: तहानलेला कावळा (The Thirsty Crow)

**Page 1:** A tired, thirsty crow standing alone in a dry, sunny landscape. Desert-like setting, dust in the air. Crow looks exhausted.

**Page 2:** The crow spots a clay pot (घडा) nearby. The crow's eyes brighten with hope.

**Page 3:** Close-up of the pot. The water level is very low inside - the crow's beak cannot reach it no matter how it tries.

**Page 4:** The crow tries different angles to drink but fails. Shows frustration and determination.

**Page 5:** The crow has an idea! Bright lightbulb moment - shows small pebbles scattered nearby on the ground.

**Page 6:** The crow begins picking up pebbles and dropping them into the pot one by one.

**Page 7:** The water level gradually rises as more pebbles go in.

**Page 8:** Finally, the water reaches the top! The crow's head is now close enough.

**Page 9:** The happy crow drinks the water, quenching its thirst. Relief and joy on the crow's face. Soft sunset colors.

## Story 3: सिंह आणि उंदीर (The Lion and the Mouse)

**Page 1:** A mighty lion sleeping peacefully under a large tree in the forest. Majestic, powerful, at rest.

**Page 2:** A tiny mouse accidentally climbs onto the sleeping lion's body. Curious little mouse, unaware of danger.

**Page 3:** The lion wakes up angry! The mouse is tiny in comparison. Shows the great size difference. Lion's eyes are fierce.

**Page 4:** The mouse is terrified, shaking. Pleading expression. The lion holds the mouse gently between paws.

**Page 5:** The lion, touched by the mouse's plea, releases it. Shows compassion and wisdom in the lion's eyes.

**Page 6:** Days pass. A hunter's rope net traps the lion. The lion struggles, captured and helpless.

**Page 7:** The tiny mouse hears the lion's roars. Runs through the forest toward the sound. Shows bravery despite small size.

**Page 8:** The mouse gnaws through the rope net with determination. Shows effort and teamwork.

**Page 9:** The lion is free! Grateful look on the lion's face. The mouse stands proud. A big and small friendship is shown.

## Story 4: लोभी कुत्रा (The Greedy Dog)

**Page 1:** A happy dog discovers a large, meaty bone on the ground. Excited, tail wagging. Shows the beautiful bone.

**Page 2:** The dog grabs the bone proudly and walks onto a wooden bridge over water.

**Page 3:** Midway on the bridge, the dog looks down and sees its own reflection in the water below.

**Page 4:** The dog's mind sees another dog (reflection) with what looks like an even better bone! Shows the dog's confusion and greed.

**Page 5:** The dog barks at the reflection, wanting the "other" bone. Shows open mouth barking.

**Page 6:** The bone falls from the dog's mouth into the water. The original bone is lost. The dog's sad, regretful face.

**Page 7:** The dog watches the bone sink into the water, now truly alone. Shows reflection is gone too. Lesson learned.

## Story 5: चतुर कोल्हा (The Clever Fox)

**Page 6:** The fox walks away into the sunset, alone. Lesson shown: sour grapes (accepting loss gracefully, though not honestly).

## How to Use These Prompts in Google Flow

1. Go to **labs.google.com/flow** (or equivalent)
2. For each story page, copy the prompt:
   - Start with: "Soft watercolor children's storybook illustration in warm, gentle colors. Hand-painted style with soft edges and emotional detail."
   - Then paste the specific page description
3. Example full prompt:
   ```
   Soft watercolor children's storybook illustration in warm, gentle colors. Hand-painted style with soft edges and emotional detail. A fast, confident rabbit with long ears running through a lush green forest. Trees and grass around him. Bright, cheerful style.
   ```
4. Generate the image
5. Download and save with the filename pattern: `story-{id}-page-{n}.png` (e.g., `story-1-page-1.png`)
6. Once all images are downloaded, I'll wire them into the website

**Total images to generate: ~170 images**
